Feb 28 - Technical Difficulties!
The radio was temporarily down while I switched software. Long story, but the short of it is, I'm switching to a different DJ automation platform. I've been beta testing it around the house, but meanwhile the old platform started to glitch out after a Windows update. This is moving my release schedule dramatically.
It's slowly coming back. The new platform is streaming. I'm currently working to get the song artist and title sent to the stream, then rework the song request database queries for the new schema. In other words, a bunch of technical crap.
New features of the new platform (Rivendell):
  • Linux Based (more stable)
  • Ability to put songs in more than one rotation
  • Ability to add songs with multiple variation cuts and play each with a weight (for example, a studio version of a song that plays 9 times out of 10, and a live version of the same song that plays 1 time out of 10)
  • Better randomness for song rotations
About
I have created a lot of mix-tape CDs for Wikiwoo over the years. This time I built her a radio station, a kind of living mix-tape. It plays in the house while she makes soap, while we drive, and while we relax at night. We Welcome anyone to enjoy along with us.
I also set up a pirate FM radio station that streams the station after seeing the 2009 Philip Seymour Hoffman movie The Boat That Rocked. One of my favorite movies was the 1990 Christian Slater movie Pump up the Voume. The FCC Part 15 compliant FM transmitter broadcasts around our house and to parts of our town.
On the tech side it's a crazy mix of technologies. Ask me about it if you're interested in learning more.
